Rules

Rounds

In each round of Poker, there will be at least one opportunity for players to bet on their hands.

Each round starts when any player makes a bet of one or more chips.

Betting

Before any of the cards are dealt, each player tosses in an initial contribution (an 'ante') of a predetermined amount into the pot.

The next player to the left must take an action, either call, raise, check or fold.

Actions

Calling means betting the same amount of chips, while raising means betting more than the call amount.

Checking has the effect of a bet of zero, but can only be done if no one has made a bet so far.

The final option is to fold (also known as a drop), which means that a player does not bet any chips, discards their hand, and is not allowed to play until the next round of cards is dealt.


A round of poker starts with two cards being dealth to each player, which only they are allowed to use.

Betting rounds end when each player has either folded, or put in exactly as much money as every other player.

  • After the round begins, the 1st betting round commences, after which 3 community cards are dealt to the middle of the table, face-up, for everyone to use.
  • After the second betting round, the 4th community card is dealt. The third betting round now begins.
  • After the third betting round, the 5th and last community card is dealt. The final round of betting commences now.
  • After the last round of betting concludes, players 'reveal' their hands - and the highest wins.

Misc

Games go on for as long as the players want - but generally a pre-set amount of rounds or time is determined before play.
